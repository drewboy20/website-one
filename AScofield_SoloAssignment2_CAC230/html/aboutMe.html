About Me

Hello! I am Andrew Scofield!

I am a student at Birmingham-Southern College and a computer science major there. You might be surprised that I am not a music major because I have decided to make a website based on music theory. Well, to give a bit of backstory to myself, music has always been a dear hobby and therapeutic pastime that I have found myself returning to more and more frequently over the past year. I have always been around music ever since I can remember. My brother is a classically trained pianist currently playing and teaching in Houston, Texas, my mother is a trained and experienced vocalist who has previously toured with several college and professional choirs, and my father grew up in the seventies music heyday when every young man felt obligated to play guitar and be in some sort of band. You could say music is in my genes.

I was not always interested in music theory and I did not always play a musical instrument. I tried to play piano growing up, but I think I was frustrated that I couldn't play Rachmaninoff within two weeks of starting! I really wish I had kept on playing even if I didn't find it to be all that entertaining because having the discipline of learning an instrument is a valuable skill to have. But the story continues from there because about 3 or 3 1/2 years ago I started veering away from modern pop songs into more "classic" music of the '60s and '70s. Mesmerized with the likes of Jimi Hendrix, Jimmy Page, and Stevie Ray Vaughn I decided I was going to teach myself how to play guitar. So I dusted off my father's 1975 Guild D25 acoustic guitar and taught myself the basic "cowboy" chords.

From that point, I caught the fever of playing an instrument and I have never turned back. A majority I have learned about music theory I have taught myself, and now I have fulfilled my mother's wish and I have joined the Birmingham-Southern concert and a cappella choirs.
